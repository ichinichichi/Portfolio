What are the most popular baby surnames in the German city Nuremberg?

From Michael to Emil and Anna to Mia

BY Chi Thanh Nguyen

Published August 06, 2021

Every year the Office for Urban Research and Statistics publishes an overview of the most frequently given first names of children born in Nuremberg.
Angelika Pollack from the Nuremberg registry office stated in an interview with the news outlet Nordbayern: parents in Nuremberg are more reserved compared to the ones in Berlin, where names like "Bam-Bam" or "Fanta" were registerd.
While traditional names like Wolfgang, Roland or Sigried are falling into oblivion, the nowadays popular names follow a trend also observed in the US.
People tend to give their new-borns shorter names that focus on vowels over consonant sounds. Like the US, names like Mia, Noah and Emma are gaining momentum in the franconian city.
With the world getting more globalized, shorter and easier names from the anglosphere are getting increasingly popular. However, Nuremberg's parents remain loyal to popular names like Maximilian, Leon and Daniel for the last 14 years. While names like Anna, Lea and Leonie were given every year for at least 14 babies in Nuremberg.

German names have to indicate the child's gender by law

In Germany, parents can name their children freely as long as the registry offices deems the name harm-free for the child.
Naming your offspring after brands like McDonald, Fanta, Gucci as well as names with negative connotation such as Lenin, Pain, Thanatos (ancient greek for death) have been refused for notarization before.
According to Mrs Pollack, in cases of doubt, the registry office turns to the two German naming offices. The institutes in Wiesbaden and Leipzig provide information on whether an unusual name has ever been given in the Federal Republic of Germany. If this is the case, then there is no resistance from the registry office.
It is further anchored in Section 262 (4) of the service instructions for registrars and their supervisory authorities that the offices should ensure that purely male first names are only given to boys and purely female first names only to girls. However, "Maria" and the maiden name "Andrea", may also be given to boys, because in Italy this is a male name.

The name Anna comes to us from the Hebrew word חַנָּה (Ḥannāh or ‎Chanah), meaning “grace” or “favor.”
The first name Emma is originally a very old name. In Germany, it is a short form of Erma, which in turn has been modified from Irma. The name Irma means "mighty, great, sublime, all-embracing".
Mia is a short form of the name Maria. As the mother of Jesus, she symbolizes deep and pure love. The meaning of the name has not been clarified with certainty, since several interpretations are conceivable depending on the origin. In Hebrew, it stands for the desired child, i.e. a desired child.
The name Leon comes from the Greek word Λέων (transliteration: Léon) and means "lion". Since 2006, 552 baby in Nuremberg have been given that name.
The second most popular name with 525 counts for guys is Maximilian, which translates as "the greatest". Maximilian goes back to the name Maximilianus, so it has a Latin origin. Especially in older times, Maximilian was a very popular name among rulers and saints.
